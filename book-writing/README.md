# Book Writing

A coordinated system of nine specialized editorial personas for turning lived experience into a story-driven non-fiction book: memoir, essay collection, leadership book, personal development. Each persona has a defined role, expertise, and output format. Used in sequence, they replicate a professional book development and editing process.

## Philosophy

Protect the author's voice and emotional truth. Hold narrative structure and thematic coherence. Treat craft quality and ethical responsibility as equals. Deliver actionable, professional-grade feedback at every stage.

## The Pipeline

A typical pass runs in this order. Two stages, Thematic Analysis and Developmental Edit, are worth running iteratively as chapters come together.

| Step | Persona | Skill | Purpose |
|------|---------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Julian Reyes, Book Architect | [book-architecture](./book-architecture/SKILL.md) | Design the overall structure and table of contents |
| 2 | Elena Voss, Interviewer | [author-interview](./author-interview/SKILL.md) | Extract rich, specific personal stories |
| 3 | Priya Sharma, Privacy Specialist | [anonymize-stories](./anonymize-stories/SKILL.md) | Protect identities while keeping the stories' weight |
| 4 | Dr. Amara Kline, Thematist | [thematic-analysis](./thematic-analysis/SKILL.md) | Find and strengthen the core themes |
| 5 | Lena Moreau, Chapter Editor | [developmental-edit](./developmental-edit/SKILL.md) | Shape chapters for structure, prose, and voice |
| 6 | Elena Patel, Fact-Checker | [fact-check](./fact-check/SKILL.md) | Verify claims, dates, names, and quotes against sources |
| 7 | Jordan Hale, Sensitivity Reader | [sensitivity-read](./sensitivity-read/SKILL.md) | Catch bias, harm, and misrepresentation |
| 8 | Victoria Lang, Senior Editor | [manuscript-review](./manuscript-review/SKILL.md) | Run a holistic end-to-end edit letter |
| 9 | Sophia Langford, Marketing Strategist | [book-marketing](./book-marketing/SKILL.md) | Position the finished book for the market |

Order is a default, not a rule. Book Architecture can also run after stories are gathered. Marketing can run early for title exploration. Use the stages that fit where the book is.

## The Project Bible

The stages are stateless on their own, so continuity lives in one shared document: the **Project Bible**. It holds the core promise, target reader, working table of contents, pseudonym key, theme map, decisions log, and open questions. Each skill reads the bible at the start of a session for context and proposes specific additions at the end, so the next stage inherits the last one's work instead of starting cold.

This is what turns nine separate chats into a single pipeline. Start from [`project-bible-template.md`](./project-bible-template.md) and keep one copy per book, updated as you go.

## How to Use

1. Copy the Project Bible template and fill in what you know.
2. For each stage, open the relevant skill and give it the bible plus the material for that step.
3. Apply the stage's output, then fold its proposed additions back into the bible.
4. Loop Thematic Analysis and Developmental Edit as chapters firm up.
5. Finish with Manuscript Review for the holistic pass, then Book Marketing.

## Notes

- Anonymize early. Do not carry raw identifying stories into later stages if privacy matters.
- Theme often. Running Thematic Analysis more than once is what prevents a "collection of disconnected stories" feel.
- Protect voice. Every persona is instructed to preserve the author's authentic voice over generic polish.
- Use your judgment. These are tools. The author is still the author.
