# Operations

Execution is not a single decision. It is the daily practice of turning decisions into production reality while the organization tries to turn them back into options.

These skills form a closed loop for that practice. They are lightweight, opinionated, and built to run with minimal overhead. The goal is not more process. The goal is momentum with eyes open.

## The Daily System

Three skills set the rhythm of a day, from outside-in context to internal focus to honest close.

[News Brief](./news-brief/SKILL.md) runs first thing. It scans the outside world for what moved on your company, products, competitors, and category, and delivers a two-minute newsreel where every story carries its "so what."

[Morning Brief](./morning-brief/SKILL.md) runs at 8 AM. It surfaces the single most important thing to move today, overnight signals that change priorities, and calendar readiness. It reads carryover commitments from the previous evening.

[Account PM Evening Briefing](./account-pm-evening-briefing/SKILL.md) runs at end of day. It delivers an opinionated account of what actually happened, what moved, what stalled, and what carries forward. It is not a neutral status report. Its carryover flags become tomorrow's morning inputs, which is what turns two briefings into one continuous thread.

[Workflow Audit](./workflow-audit/SKILL.md) runs overnight. It reviews the last 24 hours of AI assistant interactions, scores them for signal versus busywork, and surfaces recurring failure patterns. This is the meta-skill. It keeps the system self-correcting instead of slowly accumulating friction.

[Pre-Meeting Prep](./pre-meeting-prep/SKILL.md) runs on demand before any significant conversation. It compresses context, surfaces landmines, and forces clarity on the objective in under five minutes.

## The Skills

| Skill | Trigger | Primary Function | Core Discipline |
|---|---|---|---|
| [News Brief](./news-brief/SKILL.md) | On demand or scheduled | Newsreel on company, products, competitors, category | Outside-in awareness |
| [Morning Brief](./morning-brief/SKILL.md) | Scheduled (8 AM) | Daily priority, overnight signal, carryover | Focus and continuity |
| [Account PM Evening Briefing](./account-pm-evening-briefing/SKILL.md) | Scheduled (EOD) | Opinionated account status and commitments | Accountability |
| [Pre-Meeting Prep](./pre-meeting-prep/SKILL.md) | On demand | One-page brief with history, objective, risks | Clarity before conversation |
| [Workflow Audit](./workflow-audit/SKILL.md) | Scheduled (nightly) | Self-review of agent performance and friction | Continuous improvement |

Each skill follows the same self-documenting format: frontmatter (name, trigger, typed inputs, tools), an overview, a workflow or agent prompt, an output spec with a worked example, and a Lessons Learned section covering Do, Don't, Common Failures, and When to Ask.

## Philosophy

Most operational drag is not malicious. It is the predictable result of treating important work as something that should feel safe before it is allowed to move. Security reviews that outlive the model. Governance policies that block the use case leadership just approved. Endless alignment that substitutes for commitment.

These skills exist to make that visible and actionable. They favor prose over bullets because paragraphs force prioritization. They favor opinion over neutrality because neutral reporting rarely changes behavior. They favor explicit carryover and memory because yesterday's unresolved commitments are usually today's most important work.

The test of any operational system is simple: does it increase the speed and quality of decisions that actually reach production? Everything else is theater.

## Recommended Next Skills

This suite covers awareness, focus, accountability, preparation, and self-correction. Two gaps remain in the loop, both on the decision-to-production handoff. If you build two more, build these:

**Execution Lock** (post-decision or weekly). Convert decisions into locked production commitments with an owner, a date, and a definition of done. The discipline is momentum over optionality: it closes the gap where a "yes" quietly reverts to "let's revisit." Pairs with the evening briefing, which already tracks dropped balls.

**Drag Audit** (weekly or triggered). Diagnose the specific source of organizational drag on a stalled workstream: the review that outlived its purpose, the approval with no owner, the alignment loop with no exit. The discipline is confronting avoidance as a process problem, not a people problem. Pairs with Execution Lock, which sets the commitments this skill protects.

A weekly review that rolls up the daily briefings into a single accountability pass is a reasonable third addition once the daily rhythm is established.

## Connection to the Work

The same pattern that moves enterprise accounts forward is the pattern that moves platforms from pilot into production at scale. Broad interest is easy. Cautious action is the default. The scarce skill is the ability to create clarity, lock commitment, and remove the friction that appears the moment real resources and real risk are on the line.

These skills are the daily mechanism behind that work.
